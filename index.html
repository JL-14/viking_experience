Vikings in England

The Vikings arrived in the UK in 793 at Lindisfarne, where they ransacked the monastery and settlement on Holy Island. Even though there are accounts of earlier encounters with Vikings, this was the first full-scale assault with extensive plundering, slaughter, and the taking of slaves. The significance and brutality of the attack on Holy Island quickly established their reputation as fearsome warriors, and more incursions soon followed. However, the Vikings were not only bloodthirsty warriors. The Vikings brought a new civilisation to the UK, settling in large parts of England and Scotland, establishing towns and settlements right across the British Isles. The legacy of the Viking Age in the UK can be found in British culture, language, arts and crafts, placenames, and other aspects of Britain today.

Viking life and sites in Norfolk

The first account of Vikings in Norfolk dates back to 840, when Viking longboats arrived in Flegg (Scratby and Hemsby). They attacked rural towns and villages in the area before moving on to take over East Anglia, killing King Edmund of East Anglia in a battle at Hoxne is Suffolk in 869/870. As the conquest progressed across Norfolk and East Anglia, the Vikings settled down to farm and trade with the locals (and others further afield). The area eventually became known as the Danelaw (reflecting the Danish heritage of the Vikings in East Anglia), which was formalised at the Treaty of Wedmore in 886, when Danish King Guthrum converted to Christianity (and changed his name to Athelstan). Whilst there are few remaining Viking sites in Norfolk, the influence of the Vikings is evident in placenames and historical documents and artefacts. Notable towns and locations with Viking heritage include: • The Isle of Flegg • Sheringham • Thetford • Hingham • Norwich

Why Viking Experience UK?

So what was life like for the Vikings who settled in Norfolk? That is what the Viking Experience UK gives you the chance to explore. We offer experiences that include travelling along the Norfolk coast in a replica Viking longboat, travelling inland ‘raid-style’ (with light tents and equipment), life in a Viking settlement, and exploring Viking arts and crafts. The Viking Experience UK works with experts from the University of East Anglia, English Heritage, the British Museum, and the Ancient Technology Centre in Dorset to name a few, ensuring that the experience is as authentic as possible. We also have links to museums in Scandinavia, offering additional insights. We have a number of core experiences, and can offer bespoke experiences for individuals and schools (please enquire through the contact form).

Location

Our base camp is located near the Isle of Flegg, where the Vikings first set foot in Norfolk. The camp consists of our main reception, changing rooms (as you will be given Viking Age outfits to wear during the experience), and safe storage of items you do not wish to take with you. There are refreshments available for purchase at the main building, as well as a shop for souvenirs from your time as a Viking. There is ample parking on site.
